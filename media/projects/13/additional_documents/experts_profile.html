Bio

Fiona Apple McAfee-Maggart (born September 13, 1977) is an American singer-songwriter and pianist celebrated for her poetic lyrics, expressive contralto voice, and genre-blending style. She rose to prominence with her debut album Tidal (1996), which earned her a Grammy Award for Criminal. Her later albums — When the Pawn… (1999), Extraordinary Machine (2005), The Idler Wheel… (2012), and Fetch the Bolt Cutters (2020) — all received widespread acclaim, with the latter winning two Grammys. Known for her uncompromising artistry and long gaps between releases, Apple remains one of the most distinctive and influential figures in alternative music.
